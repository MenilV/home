## General Contractors

### Approach

**Option 1: General Contractor**
- One company manages all trades
- Easier for you, more expensive
- Get references, check insurance

**Option 2: Manage Yourself**
- Hire each trade separately
- Cheaper but time-consuming
- Need to coordinate yourself

### Recommended Approach

For renovation this size:
1. **Hire general contractor** for structural + rough-in
2. **You manage** finishing trades (tiles, paint, floors)
3. **Specialists** for: Heat pump, KNX, Windows

## Quotes Checklist

When getting quotes, ask for:

- [ ] Detailed breakdown (materials + labor)
- [ ] Timeline/schedule
- [ ] Payment terms
- [ ] Warranty
- [ ] References
- [ ] Insurance/liability
- [ ] What is NOT included

---

## Red Flags

⚠️ Warning signs:
- [ ] No written quote
- [ ] Cash only / no contract
- [ ] Pressure to decide immediately
- [ ] No references
- [ ] Much cheaper than others
- [ ] No warranty mentioned
